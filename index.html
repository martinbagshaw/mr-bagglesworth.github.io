About Me

I am applying for the winter 2018 cohort of Founders and Coders because I want to learn, problem solve, and collaborate on projects with similarly-minded people in the future.

I am someone who is not new to coding. When searching for Graphic Design jobs after leaving university in 2011, it seemed that more often than not, some experience with HTML and CSS would be required in the job specification. I took time to learn with online resources, got in to WordPress development, and built up a small portfolio from freelancing, before working in two full time roles that involved a mix of print and digital design, and one in which the duties were fully digital.

Over time, I have become more fond of the web development side of work for several reasons. I like the learning process, and the feeling of progression that learning a new coding language or creating something new and exciting on the web gives me. I like digging into a subject, understanding it, and working out why things are the way they are. I also find the scope of possibilities for what you can do with modern front end development quite inspiring.

So you are already a developer. Why Apply?

A common theme throughout my code learning process is that I have almost exclusively been doing it alone and in isolation, even when working as a full time employee. This was particularly the case in my last full time role, in which I had the task of working on apps and software for large touchscreens, something I was quite clear I had no prior experience in, but was hired for anyway. Unfortunately, I failed to live up to my manager’s expectations, and I did not get taken on as a full time employee after my probationary period. On the plus side, I did teach myself a lot more technically, got exposure to working in a larger company, and learnt that I most certainly do not want to work on large projects on my own in the future, especially when they are borne out of a political need, rather than necessity.

After reading up on the Founders and Coders programme and attending a couple of the meetups, I think I have found a great opportunity to get involved with a collective of people who want to help each other learn, and in an open and friendly environment. Engaging in the prerequisite materials, especially CodeWars Katas, has given me a greater insight into problem solving techniques for more complex coding problems, pushing me to think in a more structured way.

Should I get accepted on to the course, I would hope to contribute to other people’s development as much as I can, become a better problem solver, and form relationships with others on the course with a view to working together in the future. In the future, I want to be part of a working environment where people pool their skills to produce work which is beyond the level that any one individual could produce - ‘beyond the sum of their parts.’
