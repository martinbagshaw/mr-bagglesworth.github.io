My application for Founders & Coders

Hello, my name is Martin Bagshaw, a.k.a. Mr. Bagglesworth. I felt like using a pseudonym (for once in my life) when signing up to Github. I guess I have a bit of a fondness for the Austin Powers movies.

I am not a newbie to coding, having been picking up knowledge, and working on websites since 2012. As a Graphic Design graduate looking for a job, learning some HTML and CSS felt like a necessity, as did knowledge of a CMS, such as WordPress.

After building up a modest freelance portfolio, I landed my first 9-5 job, working on generally basic websites for small businesses. After around 18 months, I left for a new role, with the promise of a more varied workload, including graphic design and IT support work. Unfortunately, finding the working environment somewhat stifling and unsupportive, I left after a year, opting for a breath of fresh air: going travelling for a few months, visiting family in the USA.

Upon my return in early 2017, I took online courses with Treehouse in Web Design and JavaScript programming, alongside continuing with some freelance work, and applying for full time roles. I sought to focus more on front end development than traditional graphic design, as I was (and still am) inspired by what you can build with modern HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, something which I have come to gain more satisfaction from than tweaking designs, which often must confirm to personal tastes.

In August of 2017, I landed a new job as a Digital Designer at UCAS, with duties including but not limited to designing, maintaining, and creating apps and programs to work on touchscreen devices, something I was clear I had no experience of in my interview, but I was told I would be given time to learn. I poured a lot of time into learning the required technology and meeting with stakeholders to formulate a firm brief for an app. With narrow goalposts to aim for - that missed the main task that students need an app for, this duty was verbally taken off my list late in the year. Shortly after returning to work in 2018, I was dismissed. This came as quite a shock, for my boss told me earlier in the week that the purpose of the meeting was to 'reaffirm my objectives.' I was also close to completion on my touchscreen project, an interactive floor plan to display information to students at open day events.

A common theme throughout my code learning process (until coming along to a couple of Founders and Coders Meet-ups) is that I have largely been doing it alone, in isolation, seeing the problem through my eyes only. Aside from being a bit lonely, this is a flawed approach, one which I wish to put an end to, especially after events earlier this year. I hope to collaborate with, rather than work for others on all future projects I am involved with. I want to be in an environment where people pool their skills to produce work which is beyond the level that any one individual could produce. Last but not least, I want to work and learn with others who are keen on maintaining an open discourse, and mutually supportive environment.

Since my time at UCAS, I have done a little bit of soul searching, spending a little more time creating stencil artwork, considering other lines of work, and picking up a bit of farm work (something I have done on and off for the past decade). One thing I have not stopped doing however is thinking of and planning creative projects such as this app for climbing knots, or building things such as this app-like implementation of my climbing logbook (disclaimer: it grinds to a halt on android chrome!). This uses information supplied from here. If you had not guessed, climbing is a big passion of mine!
